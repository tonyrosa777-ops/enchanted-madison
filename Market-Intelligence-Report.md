# Enchanted Madison: Market Intelligence Report

**Prepared March 2026 | Pre-Launch Competitive Strategy for enchantedmadison.com**

---

## 1. Executive summary

**Enchanted Madison is entering a zero-competition local market with a $3.8B+ tailwind behind it.** There is no glamping property in Madison, Indiana — not one tent, dome, or treehouse. Hot tubs are explicitly described as "a rarity" in local Airbnb listings. The 600,000+ annual visitors to Madison's 130-block National Historic Landmark District and Clifty Falls State Park have exactly zero luxury-nature-immersive lodging options. This is an extraordinary market gap.

The glamping industry is growing at **9.5–12.5% CAGR** and will double to $7–10B by the early 2030s. Romance travel is surging even faster at **24.2% CAGR**, with the U.S. capturing 38% of global share. Couples aged 25–50 — Enchanted Madison's core demo — represent the majority of glamping participants and spend **$266/day on average**. Direct bookings now account for **54.7%** of glamping revenue, validating the direct-book strategy.

However, the current Canva-built website is invisible to search engines, lacks social proof, hides pricing, and creates booking friction through a third-party redirect. Competitors like Dunlap Hollow (booked 1+ year out), AutoCamp (Hilton partnership), and Camp Aramoni ($500–600/night all-inclusive) demonstrate that **exceptional branding, seamless booking UX, and premium positioning command massive demand**. The redesigned site must fix these foundational issues while leveraging Enchanted Madison's unique advantages: the only glamping in the Louisville-Cincinnati-Indianapolis triangle with private hot tubs, proposal packages, and day-use experiences near a state park and a charming historic downtown.

## 2. Target audience profile

### Primary persona: "The Intentional Romantic" (65% of revenue)

Couples aged **27–45**, dual income, living within a 2-hour drive (Louisville, Cincinnati, Indianapolis). These are milestone-driven travelers — anniversaries, proposals, birthdays, "just because" escapes. They discover stays through Instagram Reels, Pinterest boards, and Google searches like "romantic glamping near me." They value **privacy, aesthetics, and curated experiences** over DIY adventure. Average trip spend: $400–800 for a 1–2 night stay.

**Psychographic profile:** They want to feel like they discovered something special — a hidden gem, not a chain hotel. They photograph everything. They care about sustainability but won't sacrifice comfort. They read reviews obsessively and book direct when they trust the brand. They'll pay a premium for a private hot tub and a fire pit more than they'll pay for a larger room.

**Pain points:** Generic cabin rentals that look nothing like the photos. Clunky booking experiences on mobile. Hidden fees. Lack of clear information about what's included. Properties that serve families and couples interchangeably, diluting the romantic atmosphere.

### Secondary persona: "The Experience Collector" (25% of revenue)

Women aged **25–40** booking girls' nights, bachelorette pre-parties, and friend getaways. They want **Instagrammable moments, curated activities, and exclusivity**. They discover through TikTok and friend referrals. Budget: $75–150/person for a 3–4 hour experience, $200–400/person for overnight.

### Tertiary persona: "The Day Tripper" (10% of revenue)

Couples and small groups seeking **2-hour hot tub escapes, date night experiences, and proposal setups** — no overnight stay required. This persona extends the revenue per property beyond nightly rates and fills weekday gaps. Budget: $79–349/couple depending on package tier.

## 4. Individual competitor deep-dives

### Dunlap Hollow — The visual branding gold standard

**URL:** dunlaphollow.com | **Location:** Hocking Hills, OH (~3 hrs from Madison)

Dunlap Hollow operates three accommodations on a private 40-acre wooded property — a cottage ($250/night), an A-Frame ($675/night), and a sandstone cave house ($820/night) that books **more than a year in advance**. The Cave alone generates virality and demand that most properties never achieve.

**What makes it exceptional:** The photography is magazine-quality — moody, warm, atmospheric shots with no people in them, allowing viewers to project themselves into the space. The Squarespace site is minimal and elegant, with a clean nav (Accommodations, Property, Gift Cards, Mailing List, Book). The mailing list is positioned as "get notified of cancellations" — a brilliant lead capture tied to genuine scarcity. They've also diversified revenue with A-Frame architectural plans and consulting services. Their palette uses **deep forest green and brass metallic** against warm off-white backgrounds with editorial serif headings.

**Weakness to exploit:** No on-site reviews, no blog, no content marketing, no romantic experience packages. They sell accommodations, not experiences. Enchanted Madison can out-market them on storytelling, SEO content, and curated romantic experiences while learning from their visual discipline.

### Camp Aramoni — The all-inclusive model

**URL:** camparamoni.com | **Location:** Tonica, IL (~4.5 hrs from Madison)

Eleven custom South African safari tents on 96 acres of a restored 19th-century brickyard. **$500–600/night all-inclusive** — breakfast, dinner by Chef Cleetus Friedman (formerly of acclaimed Chicago restaurants), s'mores, and firewood. The 21+ age requirement immediately signals sophistication. They also host weddings in a converted 1800s blacksmith shop.

**What makes it exceptional:** The all-inclusive model justifies premium pricing and eliminates decision fatigue. The chef-driven dining program transforms "a place to sleep" into a **destination restaurant with beds**. The woman-owned, family-operated founder story adds authenticity. Heavy press coverage from Travel Agent Central, OutThere Magazine, and Secret Chicago drives organic demand.

**Weakness to exploit:** No hot tubs at individual tents. Some TripAdvisor reviews note service didn't match the price. Seasonal only (May–November). Enchanted Madison can offer a similar curated feel at a lower price point with the private hot tub advantage Camp Aramoni lacks.

### AutoCamp — Best-in-class booking UX

**URL:** autocamp.com | 9 locations with custom Airstream trailers

AutoCamp's homepage booking widget is the **gold standard for hospitality conversion design**. It uses a conversational sentence format: "I want to stay at AutoCamp — [Location] — During [dates] — With [guests]" followed by a "Check Availability" button. This reduces cognitive load and feels natural rather than transactional. The widget sits directly on the homepage with a full-screen hero video behind it.

Their visual identity is the strongest of all brands audited — **modern minimalist with mid-century soul**. Sage greens, warm whites, sandy beiges. Professional design-forward photography emphasizing the Airstream aesthetic. The Hilton Honors integration gives them access to millions of loyalty members, and they clearly communicate tier-based benefits (Silver = eco-waters, Gold = firewood, Diamond = s'mores + upgrade).

**Steal:** The conversational booking widget format. **Innovate beyond:** AutoCamp has no virtual tours, no on-site reviews, and hides pricing until deep in the booking flow. Enchanted Madison should show starting prices and reviews above the fold.

### Firelight Camps — The intimate boutique model

**URL:** firelightcamps.com | **Location:** Ithaca, NY (single location on La Tourelle Inn grounds)

Firelight operates a small glamping camp that shares the grounds with La Tourelle Inn & Spa, giving guests access to a **steakhouse, spa, sauna, and Buttermilk Falls trails** without Firelight owning or operating any of it. The brand uses a warm **burgundy/wine red** palette with cream backgrounds and warm amber accents.

**What makes it exceptional:** The homepage FAQ directly addresses first-timer anxiety ("Is there electricity? Heating? Running water?"). This transparency reduces booking friction for glamping newcomers. Complimentary inclusions — continental breakfast, campfire, trail access, bath products, and a **5–6 PM wine hour** — create extraordinary perceived value. They use WebRezPro for booking and Mailchimp for email capture.

**Steal:** The homepage FAQ model and the complimentary inclusions strategy. **Innovate beyond:** Their Squarespace site and photography are modest compared to larger brands. Enchanted Madison should match the hospitality philosophy with superior visual execution.

### Serenity Springs — Indiana's established romantic cabin brand

**URL:** serenity-springs.com | **Location:** La Porte, IN (near Indiana Dunes)

The closest Indiana-based romantic competitor, operating **30+ private cabins on 85 acres for 25+ years**. Pricing runs $270–370/night. They've perfected the couples-only positioning with whirlpool tubs, fireplaces, and a "butler door" for surprise deliveries. Add-on packages are aggressively simple: Romance Package ($50), Wine & Cheese ($50), "Kinky" Package with Kinky Vodka, boa, and whipped cream ($60).

**Weakness to exploit:** No glamping element — traditional cabins only. Dated branding. No content marketing. Located 4+ hours north. Enchanted Madison offers a more contemporary, experience-driven alternative with glamping structures, private outdoor hot tubs, and curated proposal/date-night packages that Serenity Springs has never attempted.

### Dugan Hollow Retreats — The local threat

**URL:** duganhollow.com | **Location:** Madison, IN (< 2 miles from downtown)

The most direct local competitor: an 1830s hand-hewn log cabin plus four luxury suites on 54 private acres overlooking Dugan Hollow Falls. This property shares Enchanted Madison's nature-immersive positioning and proximity to Clifty Falls.

**Weakness to exploit:** Their Wix website lacks pricing transparency, visible reviews, and content marketing. No glamping structures — just traditional cabins and suites. No curated experience packages. Enchanted Madison can differentiate with unique structures (glamping tent, bell tent, campsite), proposal/date-night experiences, and a far more polished digital presence.

### Firefly Hills Kentucky — The timeline competitor

**URL:** fireflyhillskentucky.com | **Location:** Beattyville, KY (~3.5 hrs from Madison)

Also launching **Spring 2026**, Firefly Hills offers safari tents with king beds on wooden platforms. Their Squarespace site already features exceptional content: Full Travel Guide, Food Guide, Local Attractions Guide, Outdoor Adventure Guide, Rock Climbing Guide, and Map Guides. They use NewBook for booking and offer a membership plan for repeat guests.

**Steal:** Their content-first approach and membership model. **Beat them on:** Luxury amenities — Firefly Hills tents have no hot tubs, no romance-specific packages, and their own site warns it "won't feel like home." Enchanted Madison should position as the premium romantic alternative.

### The Chandler Hotel — Madison's boutique standard-bearer

**URL:** thechandlerhotel.com | **Location:** Downtown Madison, IN

Five individually decorated rooms in a restored 1870s livery stable with the only rooftop terrace in Madison. **10.0 Expedia rating, 9.7 Kayak rating (131 reviews)**. Contactless self-check-in. The owners also operate Vintage Lanes (bowling alley/wine bar/speakeasy), creating a lifestyle brand ecosystem.

**Not a direct competitor** — they serve urban boutique, Enchanted Madison serves nature-immersive — but they prove that **premium pricing ($135–225) works in Madison** when the experience and branding are excellent. Consider a cross-promotion partnership: "Urban night at The Chandler + nature night at Enchanted" packages.

## 5. Market gap and opportunity analysis

### The five gaps Enchanted Madison fills

**Gap 1: Zero glamping in Madison.** Among the ~89 vacation rentals, 5+ B&Bs, 4+ hotels, and 71-room state park inn, not a single property offers glamping tents, domes, or nature-luxury structures. Enchanted Madison creates a new category.

**Gap 2: Hot tubs are scarce.** Airbnb listings explicitly note hot tubs as "a rarity in the area." Every successful regional glamping competitor (Dunlap Hollow, Dunlap Ridge, Antler Log Cabins) has made hot tubs standard. Enchanted Madison's private hot tubs are a genuine differentiator in the local market.

**Gap 3: No curated experience packages.** Madison's lodging providers sell beds. Nobody sells proposals, date nights, girls' nights, or hot tub escapes as bookable experiences. This is a massive whitespace. National data shows couples spend **$2,500–2,900 on proposals** (excluding the ring), and 37% of engagements happen November–February. A $399–899 proposal package captures a market that doesn't currently exist in Madison.

**Gap 4: Day-use revenue is untapped.** ONSEN Hot Tub Rentals in Oregon has operated hourly hot tub rooms profitably for **35 years** at $35–60/hour. Swimply has hosted **4+ million** hourly pool and hot tub experiences. No glamping property in the Midwest offers bookable day-use hot tub escapes. At $79–99/couple for 2 hours with 5 sessions/day, two tubs could generate **$750+/day** in incremental revenue.

**Gap 5: Louisville–Cincinnati–Indianapolis corridor is unserved.** Madison sits at the geographic center of three major metros (Louisville: 50 min, Cincinnati: 60 min, Indianapolis: 90 min) totaling **~5 million people**. No luxury glamping property directly markets to this triangle. The Hocking Hills competitors (3+ hours away) and Brown County (1.5+ hours) are more distant from Louisville and Cincinnati.

### Where competitors are weak

Across all 12 competitors audited, these weaknesses appear repeatedly and represent opportunities for Enchanted Madison to dominate:

- **No on-site review integration.** Not a single national brand (Under Canvas, AutoCamp, Collective Retreats) displays real-time review feeds on their websites. They rely entirely on external platforms.
- **Pricing is hidden.** Every major brand forces users into a booking engine before showing rates. This frustrates browsers and increases bounce.
- **No virtual tours.** Zero competitors offer 360° virtual tours or interactive property maps.
- **No AI chatbot or concierge.** Not one competitor has deployed conversational AI for guest inquiries.
- **Content marketing is weak regionally.** Only Firefly Hills (pre-launch) has invested in comprehensive destination guides. The Madison-area market has virtually no SEO content from lodging providers.
- **Proposal and date-night marketing is nearly absent.** Domance Glamping (Texas) and Bolt Farm Treehouse (Tennessee) are the only glamping brands with dedicated proposal landing pages. No Midwest competitor has this.

## 6. Trend report: glamping and romantic travel 2025–2026

### Market trajectory

The global glamping market reached **~$3.8B in 2025** and is projected to hit **$7–10B by 2032**, according to consensus estimates from Grand View Research, Fortune Business Insights, Mordor Intelligence, and four other research firms. North America represents ~$1.06B of this market. The romance travel segment specifically is valued at **$2.28B in 2025** with the U.S. capturing 38% of global share.

Gen Z and Millennial glampers (ages 18–32) account for **43.9% of revenue** and spend $266/day — nearly double the $134/day average for Boomers. The 33–50 age cohort is growing fastest at 10.2% CAGR. These two groups represent Enchanted Madison's core demographic.

### Ten trends shaping the opportunity

**1. Direct booking dominance.** Direct bookings now capture **54.7%** of glamping revenue, validating Enchanted Madison's direct-book strategy. OTA bookings are growing at 10.3% CAGR but increasingly serve as research channels rather than conversion endpoints. Travelers search on Airbnb, then book direct for better rates.

**2. AI-powered personalization.** 77% of hoteliers plan to invest in AI tools. The AI hospitality market is projected to reach $8B+ by 2033. Applications range from dynamic pricing and automated guest messaging to AI concierge chatbots that deliver **30% higher satisfaction** than rule-based alternatives. 60% of leisure travelers under 45 now use AI tools for trip planning.

**3. Instagrammable design as currency.** Social media is the #1 growth driver for glamping awareness. Properties are being designed with "photo-worthy moments" as a core feature — string lights, hot tubs under stars, fire pits with blankets. Enchanted Madison's Velvet Buck tent with string lights and woodland setting is inherently shareable.

**4. Wellness glamping.** 49% of Gen Z campers use glamping for mental health benefits. 62% of romance travelers choose spa-adjacent options. Hot tub glamping is specifically cited as a couples trend. Enchanted Madison's hot tub positioning aligns perfectly.

**5. Eco-luxury expectations.** 83% of travelers prioritize sustainability. 81% prefer eco-friendly accommodations. This doesn't mean sacrificing comfort — it means solar panels, local sourcing, Leave No Trace principles, and communicating about it prominently.

**6. Hyper-local immersion.** 67% of romance travelers seek cultural immersion. Cooking classes, wine tastings, artisan partnerships, and farm-to-table dining are expected, not exceptional. Madison's wineries (Thomas Family, Lanthier), breweries (Mad Paddle), 25+ restaurants, and historic downtown create an ecosystem no Brown County competitor can match.

**7. Day-use and micro-stay revenue.** Year-round operations and non-overnight revenue streams (hot tub escapes, date nights, experience packages) are growing as operators seek to maximize revenue per property. This is Enchanted Madison's most innovative opportunity.

**8. Pet-friendly as standard.** 50% of younger campers bring dogs. The Curated Campsite with Bell Tent already accepts dogs — this should be prominently marketed.

**9. Contactless, mobile-first operations.** 64% of internet traffic and ~70% of hotel bookings happen on mobile. Self-check-in, SMS-based communication, and digital guidebooks are expected by the target demographic.

**10. Celebration and milestone travel.** Group bookings for 7+ people are up 14%. "Buddymoons," bachelorette glamping, and destination proposals are mainstream. Enchanted Madison's proposal and girls' night packages tap directly into this trend.

## 8. Website redesign recommendations

### Branding direction

**Brand personality:** Romantic, intimate, nature-luxe, curated, slightly mysterious. The word "Enchanted" should be lived, not just named — every touchpoint should feel like discovering a secret garden.

**Recommended color palette:**

```
Primary — Deep Forest Green:    #2C3E2D  (headlines, nav, footer, primary branding)
Secondary — Warm Dusty Rose:    #C4917B  (CTA buttons, accents, hover states)
Accent — Muted Gold:            #B8965A  (icons, dividers, premium badges, highlights)
Background — Warm Cream:        #F5F0EB  (page backgrounds, card surfaces)
Body Text — Deep Charcoal:      #2D2926  (paragraphs, readable warmth)
Secondary Text — Warm Gray:     #8B8178  (captions, metadata, subtle text)
Hero Text — Warm White:         #FEFCFA  (overlay text, input fields, cards)
Dark Sections — Deep Night:     #1A2A1E  (footer, dark feature sections)
```

**Typography pairing:** Use a **refined serif for headlines** (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, or DM Serif Display) paired with a **clean sans-serif for body** (Inter, Source Sans Pro, or Lato). This combination signals editorial luxury while maintaining readability. Headlines at 48–72px desktop. Add a subtle script font sparingly for accent elements ("est. 2026," decorative labels, section dividers).

**Photography direction:** Commission professional shoots at golden hour with warm, moody tones. Show empty spaces (Dunlap Hollow approach) so visitors project themselves in. Key shots needed: hot tub under string lights at twilight, fire pit with blankets and wine, tent interior with morning light through canvas, Clifty Falls waterfall, downtown Madison storefronts, overhead charcuterie board, couple silhouette on balcony. No stock photography. Drone footage of the wooded property for hero video.

**Tone of voice:** Warm, confident, inviting, slightly poetic. Not corporate. Not overly casual. Think: a well-traveled friend who knows exactly where to take you. Example: "Where the woods hold your secrets and the stars pour your champagne."

### Hero section design

The hero should use a **10–20 second cinematic video loop** (muted, auto-playing) showing: sunset through trees → string lights turning on → steam rising from hot tub → fire pit sparks ascending → morning mist over tent → couple's hands clinking glasses. Apply a **45% dark overlay** for text readability.

**Recommended hero copy:**

> **Where Romance Meets the Wild**
> 
> Luxury glamping, private hot tubs, and curated experiences in the woods of Madison, Indiana — minutes from Clifty Falls and historic downtown.
> 
> [Check Availability]  ·  [Explore Our Stays]

Below the hero fold, immediately show:

1. **Three property cards** with photos, names, "from $XX/night," and star ratings (once available)
2. **Social proof bar:** Press logos or "★ Join 400+ couples on our VIP list"
3. **Inline booking widget** (conversational format inspired by AutoCamp)

**Alternative hero headlines to A/B test:**

- "Your Private Escape Starts Here"
- "Glamping, Hot Tubs, and Starlit Nights"
- "The Midwest's Most Romantic Outdoor Retreat"
- "Nature. Luxury. Just the Two of You."

### Booking journey UX

**Critical fix:** Replace the third-party redirect to direct-book.com with an **embedded inline booking widget** on the Enchanted Madison domain. Every click away from the main domain loses ~20% of users. Options:

- **Lodgify** ($16–40/mo) — all-in-one website builder + booking engine with embeddable widgets, channel management, and calendar sync. Best for launch phase.
- **OwnerRez** ($40/mo per property) — maximum control, robust accounting, highest Capterra ratings. Better for scaling.
- **Checkfront** ($49/mo) — specifically strong for experience/activity bookings (hot tub escapes, date nights). Consider for day-use products.

**Booking flow design (3 steps max):**

1. **Select your experience** → Visual cards (Enchanted Cottage, Velvet Buck, Bell Tent, Campsite, Hot Tub Escape, Date Night, Proposal)
2. **Choose your dates + guests** → Calendar with real-time availability and per-night pricing
3. **Add upgrades + checkout** → Romance Add-On, s'mores kit, champagne, photographer. Apple Pay / Google Pay for one-tap mobile checkout.

**Trust signals to embed in the booking flow:**

- "Book direct — best rate guaranteed" badge
- "Free cancellation up to 48 hours" (if policy allows)
- Secure checkout badge (SSL + payment processor logos)
- "What's included" expandable section on the booking page
- Micro-review quotes next to each property ("*Absolutely magical* — Sarah K.")

**Scarcity done right:** Show real availability. "Only 3 weekend dates left in October." "The Velvet Buck was last booked 4 hours ago." "Peak fall foliage weekends are 80% reserved." Never fabricate urgency.

### Content pillars and SEO strategy

Enchanted Madison needs to own four content territories that will drive organic traffic from Google and AI search tools:

**Pillar 1: "Romantic Getaways in Indiana"** — Target keywords: romantic glamping Indiana, romantic cabin Indiana hot tub, couples getaway southern Indiana, romantic things to do Madison Indiana. Create a definitive 3,000-word guide with internal links to each property.

**Pillar 2: "Madison, Indiana Travel Guide"** — Target keywords: things to do Madison Indiana, Clifty Falls State Park guide, Madison Indiana restaurants, Madison Indiana wineries. This captures top-of-funnel research traffic. Include a curated "Perfect Weekend in Madison" itinerary.

**Pillar 3: "Proposal and Special Occasion Planning"** — Target keywords: proposal venue Indiana, where to propose in Indiana, romantic proposal ideas outdoor, glamping proposal package. Dedicated /proposals/ landing page with gallery, packages, FAQ, and testimonials.

**Pillar 4: "Glamping 101"** — Target keywords: what is glamping, glamping packing list, glamping vs camping, first time glamping tips. This captures the education market and builds topical authority.

**Local SEO essentials:**

- Claim and fully optimize Google Business Profile before opening
- List on Google Vacation Rentals via channel manager
- Submit to Visit Madison Indiana, Indiana Tourism, and regional tourism boards
- Consistent NAP (name, address, phone) across all platforms
- Implement LodgingBusiness and VacationRental schema markup
- Create location pages optimized for: "glamping near Louisville," "glamping near Cincinnati," "glamping near Indianapolis"

## 9. Top 10 immediate wins

These are the highest-impact, lowest-effort actions Enchanted Madison should execute before the June 2026 launch:

**1. Migrate off Canva immediately.** The current site has zero SEO value — all content is trapped in images. Move to WordPress or Squarespace with real, crawlable text. This single change unlocks everything else. Every day on Canva is a day of lost search visibility.

**2. Embed the booking engine on-domain.** Replace the direct-book.com redirect with an inline Lodgify or OwnerRez widget on enchantedmadison.com. This eliminates trust friction and keeps users on-brand through checkout. One luxury villa operator gained **$127,000/year** from the same traffic by simplifying their inline booking process.

**3. Show starting prices on every property card.** "From $175/night" is a trust signal. Every national brand hides pricing — being transparent is a competitive advantage that reduces bounce and builds confidence. Display total price including fees before the final booking step.

**4. Launch a "Madison, Indiana" destination guide.** Write a 2,000-word "Ultimate Guide to Madison, Indiana" covering Clifty Falls, downtown dining, wineries, and seasonal events. This single page can rank for dozens of high-intent keywords and position Enchanted Madison as the local authority. Under Canvas built their entire organic strategy on destination guides.

**5. Build and launch the /proposals/ landing page.** No Midwest glamping competitor has a dedicated proposal page. Create one with package tiers, a photo gallery (use styled mockups pre-launch), an FAQ, and a contact form. Target "proposal venue Indiana" and "where to propose in Indiana." The average proposal spend of **$2,500–2,900** makes this a high-value conversion page.

**6. Upgrade the VIP SMS incentive.** Replace "early access and upgrades" with a concrete offer: "$25 off your first stay + free Madison Weekend Guide." Add a social proof counter ("Join 400+ couples"). Use Klaviyo for the opt-in to enable automated pre-stay, post-stay, and re-engagement sequences.

**7. Commission professional photography and a hero video.** Dunlap Hollow's entire brand is built on magazine-quality photography. Budget for a professional shoot at golden hour plus a 15-second drone/lifestyle video loop for the hero section. This is the single highest-ROI investment for a pre-launch hospitality brand.

**8. Claim and optimize Google Business Profile.** Do this today — even before opening. Set the opening date to June 2026. Add professional photos, complete all attributes (glamping, hot tub, fire pit, couples), and write a keyword-rich description. Verified businesses are **2× more trusted** by Google. This is free and takes 30 minutes.

**9. Document the build journey on Instagram and TikTok.** Pre-launch content showing tent setup, hot tub installation, design decisions, and property walkthroughs builds anticipation and provides social proof before reviews exist. Bolt Farm Treehouse built massive demand through construction-phase content. Aim for 2–3 Reels per week showing behind-the-scenes progress.

**10. List hot tub escapes on Swimply and Peerspace at launch.** These platforms have built-in demand for hourly hot tub rentals and generate bookings without marketing spend. Swimply alone has facilitated **4+ million** experiences. This creates a day-use revenue stream from day one and introduces the brand to a new audience that may convert to overnight guests.
