# Enchanted Collective Madison — Initial Website Audit

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Site:** enchantedmadison.com
**Booking Platform:** direct-book.com/properties/enchantedcollectivemadison
**Auditor:** Anthony

---

## Executive Summary

The Enchanted Collective has a visually compelling brand — warm, romantic, enchanted-woods aesthetic — but the website significantly underperforms its potential. The design language is beautiful in places (accommodations page, proposal page) but falls apart on the most conversion-critical pages: contact, packages, and booking. There are broken CTAs, off-brand external flows, dry info pages, and a contact page that doesn't even have a contact form. The site looks like it was built in stages without a unified UX strategy.

---

## Page-by-Page Analysis

---

### 1. Homepage / Hero
**Screenshot:** `screenshots/01-homepage-hero.png`

**What works:**
- Background forest video is atmospheric and on-brand
- "The Enchanted Collective" headline is bold and memorable
- Tagline ("Romantic Stays | Date Night | Proposals | Tent Campsites") communicates offerings clearly
- "Opening June 2026" creates urgency and anticipation

**Problems:**
- **Hero does not scroll anywhere** — no anchor links, no visual cue to scroll down, no below-the-fold content. It's a dead end.
- **"Unlock VIP Access" button goes nowhere** — looks like a CTA, behaves like decoration. Massive missed opportunity for list-building before opening.
- **"Book Now" sends users off-site** — breaks brand experience immediately; the booking page looks nothing like the main site.
- No social proof, trust signals, or teaser of what's below.

**Priority:** CRITICAL

---

### 2. Booking Page (direct-book.com)
**Screenshots:** `screenshots/02-booking-calendar.png`, `screenshots/03-booking-rooms.png`

**What works:**
- Functional calendar / availability system
- Shows individual units (Velvet Buck, Curated Campsite, etc.)

**Problems:**
- **Completely off-brand** — blue/white corporate booking UI vs. the warm earthy tones of the main site
- Jarring transition destroys the romantic, curated feeling built on the main site
- All rooms showing as unavailable for March 2026 (expected pre-opening, but still creates doubt)
- No storytelling, no photography that matches main site

**Priority:** HIGH — consider embedding or theming

### 10. Packages Page (enchantedmadison.com/packages)
**Screenshot:** `screenshots/12-packages.png`

**What works:**

## Key Opportunities

1. **VIP List Capture** — "Unlock VIP Access" should open a modal with email/phone opt-in. Pre-opening list = warm leads on day 1.
2. **Embedded Booking** — Replace off-site redirect with an embedded or styled booking widget to maintain brand continuity.
3. **Host Story** — Angela & Marc's personal story (how this came to be) would dramatically improve the About Us and homepage.
4. **Packages UX Overhaul** — Packages need card layouts with photos, clear pricing, and a path to add them at checkout.
5. **Contact Form on Main Site** — Critical for proposal inquiries, corporate buyouts, event bookings.
6. **Review Strategy** — Actively solicit Google reviews post-opening; embed Google review widget on reviews page.
7. **Homepage Scroll** — Build out sections below the hero: accommodations preview, packages teaser, proposal CTA, reviews strip, map.
8. **Local SEO Content** — Expand madison-info page with restaurants, events, and seasonal content.
